In Japan, many of our individual values heavily depend on our ability to conform and to 'fit in' with the crowd.

Many find it difficult or unable to follow this culture, making them feel completely useless to the world.

As a result of this, it is estimated that there are over 1 million modern-day hermits in Japan, who seek total withrawal from society, known as Hikikomori (ひきこもり).
